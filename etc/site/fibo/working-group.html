FIBO Teams

FIBO Team Chair(s) Description
FIBO Leadership Team (FLT) Robert Trypuz FLT Team is a closed group. It consists of FIBO ontologists, conceptual modelers and semantic web product experts who have been involved in defining the FIBO "Build-Test-Deploy-Maintain" methodology. The FLT manages the FIBO repository, adjudicates changes in GitHub and JIRA, ensures the alignment of FIBO with existing standards, maintains the FIBO process flow and ensures technical governance for the standard.
FIBO Process Team (FPT) Dean Allemang FPT Team is a closed group. They are responsible for establishing and maintaining FIBO infrastructure. This includes management of FIBO namespaces, GitHub and collaboration mechanisms, liaison with vendors and other mechanisms needed for automatic testing. The FIBO Process Team is integral to implementation and enhancement of the FIBO Build, Test, Deploy and Maintain (BTDM) methodology that governs the ontology.
FIBO Foundations Content Team (FND Team) Mike Bennett FND Team is responsible for designing the basic building blocks of FIBO. FIBO Foundations deals with the underlying concepts of contracts, agents, agreements, transactions, processes, dates, time, goals, legal concepts including jurisdiction and capacity, organizational types, the meaning of ownership and control, the concept of parties and roles, baseline concepts associated with products and services, the fundamental accounting concepts, units of measure, quantities and schedules. FIBO Foundations is the essential scaffolding upon which the other FIBO modules are constructed. Apply to join team
FIBO Business Entities + Derivatives Content Team (BE/DER Team) David Newman BE/DER Team is responsible for one of the content foundational components of the ontology. It addresses concepts related to the types of entities that exist across the financial system including corporations, functional entities, partnerships, private limited companies, sole proprietorships, trusts, government entities and distinct types of legal entities. FIBO BE/DER also focuses on the concepts of ownership and control covering the types of parties that exist along with expression of their capacities and powers. Moreover, FIBO BE/DER is a baseline domain ontology defining equities (equity instruments, depository receipts, equity issuance, limited partnership equity, shareholder rights and shareholder equity), bonds (concepts relating to cash debt instruments, listings, tax treatment, debt guarantees, parity variants, participation notes and cash flow), money markets (REPOs, term deposits and short term debt), and other securities (baskets, parametric schedules, pools, securities classification, securities identification, issuance, listings, restrictions and assets). Apply to join team
FIBO Financial Business & Commerce + Securities Team (FBC/SEC Team) Elisa Kendall & Richard Beatch FBC/SEC Team is responsible for one of the structural components of the ontology. The specification provides a model of business concepts that are common to all financial services including products and services, financial instruments, market types, financial intermediaries, registration authorities and regulators. These common concepts are linked to many other financial contract domain and process areas in FIBO. Moreover, FBC/SEC is a baseline domain ontology defining equities (equity instruments, depository receipts, equity issuance, limited partnership equity, shareholder rights and shareholder equity), bonds (concepts relating to cash debt instruments, listings, tax treatment, debt guarantees, parity variants, participation notes and cash flow), money markets (REPOs, term deposits and short term debt), and other securities (baskets, parametric schedules, pools, securities classification, securities identification, issuance, listings, restrictions and assets). Apply to join team
FIBO Indices and Indicators (IND Team) Elisa Kendall IND covers concepts relating to forms of market indices (including baskets, credit and debt indices), economic indicators, economic measures, market-based interest rates and foreign exchange as well as ontologies relating to common interest rates, inter-bank lending and rates of debt instruments. Apply to join team
FIBO Ratings (Ratings FCT) Cory Casanave The Ratings FCT is working to expand the concepts associated with market and credit ratings. Apply to join team

FIBO JIRA

To become part of the team, you will need to have a GitHub user (you can request to connect to FIBO using an existing user name) and you must request to have access to FIBO JIRA. Links are below:

EDMC GitHub The EDM Council GitHub repositories All FIBO content and tooling are here. Some of it is open and some is private. You will need to be logged in as a GitHub user to be able to get access.
JIRA The FIBO issue manager There is JIRA project for every FIBO domain and for every FIBO team. It is necessary to request to be added to JIRA. Learn about JIRA .
Wikis There is a wiki for every FIBO Domain and Team. These wikis correspond to the JIRA projects When a person is added to JIRA, he or she is automatically connected to the corresponding wiki. Learn about the Confluence software used to manage the FIBO wikis.

For more information on any of the above, please contact. rtrypuz@edmcouncil.org.
